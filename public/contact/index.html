Contact

2 Mins read

“I don’t do small talk. But I do deep technical conversation.”

If you’re here, you probably have something worth saying. I’m reachable through the channels below — pick whichever fits your context.

When to Reach Out

I’m open to:

  • Vulnerability research collaborations
  • Kernel security discussions and code review
  • Offensive security consulting engagements
  • CTF team invitations and writeup exchanges
  • Speaking at security conferences or community events
  • Feedback on my published research or tools

I’m not the right contact for:

  • Bug bounty handoffs on platforms I’m not registered on
  • Generic “how do I get into hacking” questions — read the blog first
  • Unsolicited recruiting without reading my work first

Where to Find Me

🐦 Twitter / X

The fastest way to reach me for quick exchanges, research discussions, or to just follow along.

@thecyberdevhq

🐙 GitHub

Where my tools and research code lives. Open issues on relevant repos for project-specific questions.

gitlab.com/yourcyberdev

✉️ Email

For serious inquiries — consulting, collaboration proposals, or vulnerability disclosures.

cyberdev@proton.me

PGP key available on request. For sensitive disclosures, always encrypt.

Vulnerability Disclosures

If you’ve found something and want a second pair of eyes, or you want to discuss responsible disclosure on a finding — email is the right channel. I take security research seriously and will respond thoughtfully.

Please include:

  • Affected software and version
  • Reproduction steps or PoC
  • Your timeline expectations

Response Time

I read everything. I don’t always reply fast — kernel debugging doesn’t pause for notifications. But if your message is worth a response, it will get one.

Average response time: 24–72 hours depending on current research load.
